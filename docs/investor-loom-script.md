## 1 · The single job of this video

Convince a warm investor, in under five minutes, that MyRhythm is not "another productivity app for people with brain fog" — it is the distribution wedge into a broken transition of care that no incumbent owns. Front-load the Founding Member number and the LOI target so a skim-viewer gets the thesis in 45 seconds.

Do **not** demo more than one flow. Do **not** show a deck.

## 3 · Verbatim script

### 0:00–0:45 · The Discharge Cliff

> "Every year in the UK alone, 350,000 people are admitted to hospital with a brain injury. On discharge day, most of them are sent home with a two-page leaflet and a follow-up appointment six weeks later. That six-week gap is what clinicians privately call **the discharge cliff**. It's where recovery is won or lost — and no software company is in the room. I'm [Founder]. I built MyRhythm because my husband walked off that cliff, and I couldn't find a single tool that met him where he actually was."

### 0:45–1:30 · The Clinical-Ready vs Life-Ready Gap

> "Here's what's on the market. Notion. Todoist. Google Calendar. Every one of them assumes an **ideal brain** — one that can hold five things in working memory, remember to open the app, and read six-point instructional text. A brain-injury survivor on day three at home can do none of that. The clinical system declared them ready to go home. Real life did not. That is the **Clinical-Ready vs Life-Ready Gap**, and it is invisible on every existing productivity roadmap."

### 1:30–2:30 · The Ideal-Brain Assumption + TAM

> "And this is not a rare-disease play. Brain injury is the wedge. Behind it: 6.5 million adults with ADHD in the UK and US. 900,000 with dementia or mild cognitive impairment in the UK alone. 74% of working adults report cognitive overload driven by stress and sleep debt. Every single one of them is failed by the same **ideal-brain assumption**. The addressable population in our two launch markets is over 60 million adults. We are building for the hardest case first — because if it works there, it works for everyone."

### 2:30–3:30 · Product demo — the Discharge Bridge Kit

> "This is what we ship. The Discharge Bridge Kit. A clinician can hand this printed one-pager to a patient on the ward — [show handout page]. When they get home, they open the app to a seven-day path — [show `/launch/discharge-bridge`]. Day one: invite one person to your Support Circle. Day two: one small voice capture. Day three: commit to *one* thing tomorrow. Not five. One. And on day seven, we generate a handover PDF the survivor brings to their first appointment — [show export]. That's the loop: **Capture, Commit, Calibrate, Celebrate**. Built on a Collaborative Cognitive Continuity layer. Externally: **Memory-First Design**."

### 3:30–4:15 · Traction

> "Today, [N] paying Founding Members. Weekly-active rate above 40%. Our first signed clinical letter of intent from [redacted NHS trust / rehab centre], with two more in conversation. We are on track for 100 members and three LOIs by 1 October. The Founding Edition is priced at £10 a month and it is selling without paid ads."

### 4:15–5:00 · Moat + the Ask

> "The moat is not the software. The moat is being the object the clinician hands over. Once we're in that hand-over moment, we are inside a distribution channel no consumer productivity app can copy. We are raising a [£X] pre-seed round to fund three clinical pilots, one full-time clinical-partnerships hire, and the v0.2 build. If you want to see the wedge before anyone else does — reply to the email this video came in. Thank you."

## 4 · Recording checklist

- Record at 1920×1080. Screen zoom 125% for demo segments so buttons are legible on phone playback.
- External mic (any USB condenser). Test 30 seconds and listen back before the real take.
- Webcam framing: eyes at upper third, plain wall behind, one warm lamp off-camera.
- No slide transitions. Cut straight from webcam to screen share.
- One take. If you fluff a line, keep going. Investors trust a founder who recovers on camera.
- Thumbnail frame: the moment you say "the discharge cliff" — freeze that face.
- Cite only the numbers already in `src/config/appDescription.ts` (`THIRD_PROBLEM_STATS`). Do not invent new stats on camera.
- Replace every `[bracketed placeholder]` before recording. Print the script and hold it just below the webcam.

---

## 5 · What this Loom is not

- Not a demo of every feature. If you show more than the Discharge Bridge, cut it.
- Not a deck read-out. If you find yourself reading a slide title aloud, cut it.
- Not a founder-story documentary. The husband story is one line in segment 1. That's the whole allocation.
- Not evergreen. Re-record when LOI 1 → LOI 2 → LOI 3 land. Each new signature is a new take.
