## 1. Purpose statement

MyRhythm exists to close three failures in how the world supports cognitive recovery and cognitive health:

1. **The Discharge Cliff** — patients leave hospital or clinic with a folder of instructions and no living system to carry them through the first 90 days at home, when relapse and re-admission risk peak.
2. **The Clinical-Ready vs Life-Ready Gap** — clinical rehab makes people well enough to leave the ward; it does not make them ready to run a household, a family calendar, a job, or a relationship on a variable-energy brain.
3. **The Ideal-Brain Assumption** — mainstream productivity, calendar, and habit apps assume a healthy, high-executive-function brain. They fail people with TBI, stroke, dementia/MCI, ADHD, long-COVID cognitive symptoms, chronic stress, and caregivers running on empty.

Every product decision, roadmap trade-off, and marketing sentence must trace back to one of these three failures. If it doesn't, we don't ship it.

## 2. The three failures — evidence base

### 2.1 The Discharge Cliff
- Stroke survivors: ~30% experience a preventable hospital readmission within 90 days; UK NHS data flags cognitive-support gaps as a leading contributor.
- TBI: ~40–60% of moderate/severe TBI patients report unmet needs at 6 months post-discharge (Headway UK, Brain Injury Association of America).
- Discharge planning in most systems ends at the ward door; there is no continuity layer between clinician-issued instructions and daily life at home.

### 2.2 The Clinical-Ready vs Life-Ready Gap
- Clinical outcomes (Barthel, GCS, MoCA) measure medical stability, not life-readiness (running a home, returning to work, managing relationships).
- Family-mediated rehabilitation studies (Cochrane, 2016+) show family involvement significantly improves functional outcomes but is under-supported by tooling.
- "No one walks alone" — recovery is a Support Circle activity, not a solo user activity.

### 2.3 The Ideal-Brain Assumption
- ADHD: ~5% of adults globally (WHO); most productivity apps punish variable executive function.
- Dementia / MCI: 55M people with dementia globally, projected 139M by 2050 (WHO, 2023).
- Long-COVID cognitive symptoms: 10–30% of post-COVID patients report persistent cognitive effects (Lancet, NIH RECOVER).
- Chronic stress & burnout: post-pandemic burnout rates in healthcare workers exceed 40% (WHO).
- Caregiver burden: ~1 in 4 UK adults provide unpaid care; 40%+ report clinical-level stress (Carers UK).

## 4. Competitive landscape

| Competitor | What they do well | Where they fall short (MyRhythm's edge) |
|---|---|---|
| **Constant Therapy** | Clinician-prescribed cognitive exercises | No life scaffolding, no Support Circle, no calendar continuity |
| **BrainHQ / Posit Science** | Evidence-based cognitive training | Exercise-only; nothing between sessions |
| **Lumosity / Elevate / Peak** | Consumer brain games, gamified | Entertainment, not recovery; no clinical bridge |
| **Cogmed** | Working memory training | Narrow scope, clinician-gated |
| **Inflow (ADHD)** | ADHD coaching, CBT-based | Single audience, no family layer |
| **Nerva / MahanaIBS** | Digital therapeutic model (chronic condition) | Different condition; validates prescription-DTx market |
| **Noom (weight)** | Assessment → personalised plan → coaching | Different domain; validates assessment-before-paywall model |
| **Headspace / Calm** | Mindfulness at scale | Not cognitive recovery; no discharge bridge |

**MyRhythm's defensible edge:**
- **Memory Bridge** — voice-first capture → ACT extraction → shareable next-steps → clinician-ready PDF export. Nothing on the market bundles this end-to-end.
- **Support Circle** — recovery as a coordinated family activity, not a solo app.
- **Bridge Pathway** — 6-stage pre- and post-discharge on-ramp; competitors start post-discharge only.
- **4C loop on a Collaborative Cognitive Continuity layer** — an operating model, not a feature list.

## 5. Evidence base for what's inside the app

- **Gratitude / Celebrate practice** — Emmons & McCullough (2003) and follow-ups: sustained gratitude journaling improves mood, sleep, and perceived support. Especially relevant to caregivers and MCI populations.
- **Spaced retrieval** — Camp et al., evidence for memory support in early dementia.
- **Energy-matching / pacing** — chronic-fatigue and post-concussion literature: matching cognitive load to available energy reduces symptom flare-up.
- **Family-mediated rehabilitation** — Cochrane 2016 review: family involvement improves stroke recovery outcomes.
- **Discharge planning** — NICE guidance NG27: structured, continuous discharge planning reduces readmission.

## 8. What MyRhythm is NOT

- Not a medical device.
- Not a diagnostic tool.
- Not a treatment.
- Not a "brain OS", "operating system for your brain", "second brain", or "brain gym" (banned external language).
- Not a solo productivity app.
- Not a replacement for clinical care — a bridge to and from it.
