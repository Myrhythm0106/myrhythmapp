# MyRhythm Founding Edition (v0.1) — Cognitive-Accessibility Audit

**Audit date:** 20 June 2026
**Scope:** the 9 Founding Core routes (`home, capture, commit, calibrate, memory, calendar, support, settings, profile`) plus the public funnel (`/`, `/mvp/user-type-selection`, `/mvp/assessment`, `/mvp/payment`, `/auth`).
**Why this audit:** ensure every screen is safe and useful for someone with memory or cognitive challenges, *and* universally valuable (a tired parent, a busy professional, an older adult — not only brain-injury survivors).

---

## Cognitive Load Rubric

Each route is scored against ten rules. **Pass / Soft-fail / Hard-fail.**

1. **One decision per screen** — max 3 primary options visible; secondary actions tucked under "More".
2. **Plain language** — no jargon ("synthesise", "calibrate", "extract" need plain sub-labels).
3. **Re-entry safety** — leaving mid-task and returning shows "Here's where you were", not a blank state.
4. **Output legibility** — body ≥ 18px, line-height ≥ 1.5, max 70ch, no all-caps blocks.
5. **Copy / amend / send affordances** — every output has Copy + Edit + Email/Share.
6. **Touch targets ≥ 56px** on primary CTAs (44px minimum for secondary).
7. **Reversibility** — destructive actions confirm; Undo toast where possible.
8. **No medical claims** — disclaimer where the surface touches symptoms, energy, or clinical export.
9. **Energy-honest defaults** — nothing demands the user during Pause/low-energy stage.
10. **Universally useful** — copy works beyond brain-injury context.

Severity key: 🟥 Critical (blocks weekend test) · 🟧 Important (fix before cohort) · 🟨 Nice-to-have (v0.2).

## Universal-value confirmation

After fixes above land, the product reads as: *"A calm place to capture conversations, decide one next step, and look back without the weight of remembering it all."* That value-line works for:
- A brain-injury survivor (the founding cohort).
- A caregiver tracking a parent's clinic visits.
- A working parent juggling appointments and school logistics.
- An older adult who wants a private record of family calls.
- A busy professional who wants meetings turned into actions.

The same surfaces serve all five — no separate "lite" mode required.
